Dearest,

Once upon a time, moonstruck by your presence -a presence like the soft consolations of the sea, steady and vast and impossibly gentle- I was held captive and I have never once wanted to escape. If fate is ever kind (though I hardly dare to hope it listens), I hope in all our red strings, their infinite tangles and frayed edges and knots tied by hands we never met, there exists a theory we haven't yet proven: that threads can stretch not just across space but across lifetimes. That somewhere, in the weft and warp of time, this same red string binds us again. And in this lifetime, I get to be with you.

And if fate is not listening then know that when I am six feet under and the maggots reach for my heart, they will find nothing left but my overflowing love for you. It will have outlasted everything else. It will have become everything else.

I know wanting something this much is asking for the universe to laugh. The yearning grew way too loud, and jinxing it is my worst fear. The thought of being separated from you makes the mundane world more melancholic—I am homesick for the heaven I found in you, and no other place has ever felt like shelter. But for you, I will do it scared. I will lay my wanting bare, even as my hands tremble. Because you are worth every risk I never thought I'd take.

I am writing this not because I have something new to say, but because the old things refuse to fade. One hundred and fifteen days later, and I still love you just as much as I did last October. I love you without end. I don't think you understand how beautiful you make my world just by living in it—how your existence rearranges the light. I admit I miss you terribly. The world is too loud without the quiet of you in it. Too flat. Too pale. Too much, and yet not nearly enough.

As long as I am alive, there will always be someone who is proud of you in everything. In the small victories and the silences and the days you only survived. Someone who watches the way you move through this world and thinks: there. there is the person I belong to.

You are the outline of warmth stitched into everything gentle. And I will say this again: no one knew how much violence it took for you to be gentle, but thank you. Thank you for surviving it. Thank you for becoming this. Thank you for being the person you are right now.

I am counting the hours until I am moonstruck again.

Until then, I keep your heartbeat in my ears like a seashell. Until then, I wear this wanting like your jacket I have no plans on returning. It still smells like you. I still sleep with it bundled against my chest.

I hope we will see each other again soon.

I’ll be here, waiting for your hug.

I remain, ever yours,
Yazzie ♡
